# Problem Statement – PhishGuard

## Background
Phishing remains one of the most common and effective forms of cyber attack affecting both individuals and organizations.

Attackers commonly use deceptive emails, text messages, and malicious links to impersonate trusted entities in order to steal credentials, distribute malware, or commit financial fraud.

Despite increased cybersecurity awareness, many users still struggle to distinguish legitimate communications from phishing attempts.

## Problem Definition
Many users lack the cybersecurity knowledge necessary to confidently identify suspicious communications before interacting with them.

This creates several risks, including:

- Credential theft
- Malware infection
- Financial fraud
- Data exposure
- Identity compromise

The problem is especially significant for non-technical users who may not recognize common phishing indicators such as suspicious URLs, urgent language, spoofed domains, or deceptive messaging.

## Proposed Solution
PhishGuard proposes a web-based cybersecurity awareness platform that allows users to submit suspicious messages or URLs for analysis.

The system evaluates phishing indicators and provides:

- Risk classification
- Explainable threat reasoning
- Practical cybersecurity guidance

This allows users to make safer decisions before interacting with suspicious content.

## Importance
Phishing attacks continue to evolve and remain highly successful due to social engineering techniques that exploit user trust and urgency.

Improving phishing awareness and providing user-friendly analysis tools can reduce successful attacks and strengthen cybersecurity decision-making.
